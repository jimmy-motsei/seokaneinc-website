The immediate priority is deploying the new Next.js site. Every week the old site remains live is a week of lost digital credibility, missed search rankings, and uncaptured leads.

## Quick Wins (This Week)

These require no redesign — they are copy, configuration, and account changes only.

1. **Replace rotating hero with a single static headline.** Write: *"Legal counsel with executive-level commercial insight — serving South African businesses for 25 years."* Follow with a sub-headline: *"Director City Seokane brings two decades of C-suite experience to every client engagement."* This change alone passes the 5-second test and deploys the firm's primary differentiator.

2. **Add a phone number and CTA above the fold.** Place `+27 (0)83 778 9288` and a "Book a Consultation" button visibly in the header. Law firm research consistently shows that making a phone number findable in under 3 seconds is the single highest-impact change for professional services sites.

3. **Claim and optimise the Google Business Profile.** Go to google.com/business and verify the listing for Seokane Inc. at 1 Maxwell Drive, Sunninghill. Add all 5 practice areas as services, upload 3–5 professional photos, set opening hours, and add the firm description. Without this, the firm is invisible in Google Maps and the local 3-pack for every geo-qualified legal query.

4. **Update the copyright year and remove third-party agency credit.** The © 2016 footer is visible to every visitor and signals the site has been abandoned for a decade. Update to © 2026. Remove the "Blue Flame Design" credit, which is unprofessional on a client-facing legal site.

5. **Fix the title tag and meta description immediately.** Current title is likely generic ("Seokane Inc." or similar). Change to: *"Commercial Litigation & Employment Law Attorneys | Johannesburg | Seokane Inc."* Add a meta description: *"Boutique law firm in Sunninghill, Johannesburg, specialising in commercial litigation, corporate law, and employment matters for South African SMEs. Founded 2001."* This alone improves every organic search result snippet.

6. **Add LocalBusiness + LegalService JSON-LD schema.** Paste the following JSON-LD block into the site's `<head>`. Google uses this for the knowledge panel and rich results. No design work required — paste into the HTML template.

7. **Request 5 Google reviews from existing clients.** Send a direct review link by email or WhatsApp. Law firms with 10+ Google reviews significantly outperform zero-review firms in the local pack. One review request email to your best five clients costs nothing and can move the firm's local ranking within 2–4 weeks.

8. **Add Open Graph meta tags.** `og:title`, `og:description`, and `og:image` are missing. WhatsApp and LinkedIn share previews currently show a blank or broken link. In South Africa, WhatsApp is a primary B2B referral channel — this is a silent conversion killer every time someone shares the site URL.

1. **Launch a legal content hub (/insights or /blog).** Publish 2–4 articles per month targeting long-tail queries that SA SME owners actively search: *"how to handle unfair dismissal in South Africa"*, *"CCMA process for employers"*, *"CIPC compliance checklist for small business"*, *"commercial contract red flags South Africa"*. 3. **Build a structured referral partner network.** Establish formal referral relationships with chartered accountants, business coaches, Johannesburg chamber of commerce representatives, and SEDA advisors who serve the same SME market. 4. **Execute a local citation campaign.** Ensure consistent Name, Address, Phone (NAP) data on: FindanAttorney.co.za, Snupit, Yellosa, Yelp SA, and the Legal Practice Council's public register. Consistent citations build local search authority and move the firm up in Google Maps rankings for "attorneys near me" queries from Sunninghill, Sandton, and Midrand.

5. **Position City Seokane as a public SME authority.** Target speaking opportunities at SME conferences (SA SME Summit, Johannesburg Chamber events), submit op-eds to Business Day or the Daily Maverick Business section on employment law or corporate compliance for small businesses, and contribute to SARS and CIPC guidance discussions. This earned media creates inbound enquiries that no paid campaign can replicate at equivalent trust levels.

Its absence is the most costly marketing decision visible in this audit.

Social proof is completely absent. No testimonials, no named clients, no case outcomes, no quantified results. For a high-stakes, high-trust purchase category like legal services, social proof is not a decorative addition — it is functionally required for conversion.

The firm has no content programme. No blog, no articles, no guides. This means the firm generates zero organic search traffic for the high-intent queries that SME decision-makers type when they have a legal problem and need a firm.

### Conversion Optimization Analysis
**Score: 18/100**

The site is structurally incapable of converting visitors who are not already fully committed to making contact. There is no CTA in the hero, no phone number visible without navigating to Contact Us, no form embedded on the page, and no urgency or value framing anywhere.

Rotating hero carousels are consistently shown in CRO research to reduce conversion rates by fragmenting attention and preventing any single message from landing. They are a pattern from early 2010s web design that has since been demonstrated to harm performance.

The contact page exists but requires deliberate navigation — for a high-intent visitor arriving from a search query, any additional step between arrival and contact is a drop-off risk. Industry benchmarks for law firm sites suggest 30–40% of contact-page-intending visitors leave without converting when there is friction in the path.

The mobile experience is likely poor. Rotating banners, navigation-only contact, and no sticky CTA bar mean mobile visitors — who now represent 60%+ of first-time visits — face the most friction at the highest-intent moment.

### SEO & Discoverability Analysis
**Score: 18/100**

The site architecture is the root cause of the SEO crisis. A single-page / hash-routing structure means Google indexes one URL and cannot crawl or rank individual practice areas. All subpage URLs (e.g. /about, /practice-areas, /services) return HTTP 404 to external crawlers, including Googlebot. Every practice area that Seokane operates in — commercial litigation, employment law, corporate law, estates, business compliance — has zero dedicated ranking opportunity.

No schema markup is implemented. LocalBusiness, LegalService, and Attorney structured data are the standard signals Google uses to surface law firms in the knowledge panel, Google Maps, and rich results. Their absence is both a ranking and a conversion disadvantage.

Google Business Profile is either unclaimed or unoptimised. This is the highest-priority single action available: a verified, fully populated GBP with practice areas, photos, and reviews is the fastest path to local 3-pack visibility for geo-qualified searches like "attorney Johannesburg" or "employment lawyer Sandton."

Core Web Vitals are at high risk on a decade-old site. Unoptimised images, render-blocking scripts, and no performance budget likely produce LCP scores above 4 seconds — well above the 2.5-second threshold at which Google applies ranking penalties under page experience signals.

The new Next.js site in this repository resolves the majority of these structural SEO problems — individual URLs per practice area, next/image for optimisation, SSG for page speed, and a clean HTML structure for schema injection. Deploying it is the highest-leverage SEO action available.
